troff resources

I've been on a quest to reduce my dependence on LaTeX. This isn't because LaTeX produces "bad" looking documents, on the contrary they are usually quite beautiful. The "problem" is that the MacTex or TexLive distributions are quite large and compiling documents is rather slow. groff is able to do most of what I want and is already available on my Linux/FreeBSD/OS X systems. But the groff documentation is a little sparse, so additional resources are required.

Unix Text Processing
Does a pretty good job at being an introductory text into troff, tbl, eqn, pic and macros
Heirloom Nroff/Troff User's Manual
This is more of a reference document, but it does have a good tutorial section as well
MOM macros
This is a "modern" macro set, and in general it does a very good job producing PDFs
